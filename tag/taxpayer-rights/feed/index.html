You innocently walk over to your mailbox. There are usual bills and flyers, and… an unexpected notice from the IRS.

Argh!

You feel your stomach sink. Your heart is pounding out of your chest and your knees go weak.

Something about that IRS logo on a number 10 envelope that sends people into a panic!

The first thing to do?

Take a deep breath.

The Protection and Fundamental Rights You Have with the IRS: Yes, They Exist!

The IRS is not going to show up at your door. They will not come for you at 3 am. They will not call you every five minutes at work or blow up your cell phone with hundreds of texts and emails.

Consider this first letter a conversation opener.

Yes, you will have to respond. But you don’t have to be paralyzed by fear.

For some reason, people can feel helpless when it comes to the IRS. It’s as if their constitutional rights evaporated the moment they opened that mailbox.

The Protection and Fundamental Rights You Have with the IRS: The Taxpayer Bill of Rights 

Whether you know it or not, it’s just not true that you are powerless against the IRS.

Not only are all your rights still intact, but the IRS goes one step farther in ensuring you are treated fairly.

They have their own Bill of Rights, called the Taxpayer Bill of Rights.

Included in this Taxpayer Bill of Rights is the right to appeal IRS decisions and to hire someone to represent you.

The bottom line is you don’t have to face this alone. You needn’t fear that you will be stalked like a hunted animal, or that you can’t fight IRS’s allegations against you.

The IRS must abide by the Taxpayer Bill of Rights.

So relax and open that notice. It’s not the end of the world. You can survive (even thrive) after this!
