Getting a Love Letter from the Internal Revenue Service

Got a notice from the Internal Revenue Service?

Don’t stick it in a drawer.

Or worse, throw it away.

After the initial shock wears off, you shouldn’t forget to actually open the notice. You’d be surprised at how many people don’t!

Getting a love letter from the Internal Revenue Service is not a cause for immediate panic.

The only thing to fear is fear itself.

You need to confront the problem and resolve it… because if you don’t, things can deteriorate. It’s better to face the music rather than let things spiral out of control!

Getting a love letter from the Internal Revenue Service? Know that there are many different kinds of notices that the IRS sends.

Each IRS notice means something different. Read carefully to understand what the IRS is communicating with you and what your response should be.

That’s the key… the IRS is expecting a response.

And you will be given a certain amount of time to do that.

Getting a love letter from the Internal Revenue Service? Ignoring it is the worst possible thing you could do!

Listen, the IRS can make a mistake. If that’s the case, it can be cleared up.

Even if you do owe back taxes, it’s not the end of the world.

You or your tax representative can provide the documentation needed to justify your return. Or you can put together an installment plan to pay over time. You can even file an Offer in Compromise, where the IRS agrees to settle for less than the full amount of tax owed. All of these options put you on the road to recovery of your good standing with the IRS.

On the other hand, you can make things worse by ignoring those notices altogether. The penalties and interest build over time.

So do yourself a big favor.

Sit down, take a deep breath, and open that IRS notice.
